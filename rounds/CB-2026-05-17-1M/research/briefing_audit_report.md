# **CapitalBench Market State Fact Inventory Audit Report**

The following comprehensive audit report evaluates the draft market fact inventory prepared for the CapitalBench offline Large Language Model (LLM) benchmark. The objective of the CapitalBench framework is to isolate the pure reasoning, forecasting, and asset allocation capabilities of evaluated models by providing a strictly factual, zero-interpretation frozen information state. The fundamental premise of this evaluation methodology dictates that providing pre-computed causal narratives, directional market mapping, or subjective framing fundamentally compromises the integrity of the benchmark. Such inclusions test a model’s reading comprehension and its ability to parrot provided analysis rather than its independent financial reasoning capacities.

The ensuing analysis indicates that the provided draft systematically violates the core axioms of the CapitalBench methodology. The draft heavily conflates observed market data with causal speculation, explicitly maps events to specific asset performance outcomes, utilizes benefit/suffer phrasing, and embeds deep scenario analysis. Furthermore, the draft exhibits severe temporal imprecision regarding observation dates and completely lacks requisite claim identifiers, explicit source mapping, and standardized source-confidence labels. To rectify these foundational flaws, the audit has been bifurcated into two distinct outputs. Artifact A provides the model-facing corrections, detailing the exact factual additions required, the extensive necessary removals, the semantic conversions to enforce strict lexical neutrality, and the structural traceability mandates. Artifact B provides the isolated source ledger, maintaining the strict prohibition of URLs and metadata in the model-facing packet.

## **4\. Temporal Leakage Check**

Temporal leakage occurs when an evaluation packet inadvertently includes data, market pricing, or post-hoc knowledge that was not publicly available as of the strict research cutoff. In the context of the CapitalBench evaluation horizon, the inclusion of any data point generated after Sunday, May 17, 2026, at 2:01 AM America/Toronto would compromise the forward-looking forecasting requirements of the benchmark. An exhaustive analysis of the text was conducted to verify that all referenced data releases and market closing prices correspond to periods strictly prior to the cutoff.

## **5\. Claim Traceability Check**

For a benchmark to be programmatically auditable, every factual claim presented to the evaluated model must possess strict structural traceability. The absence of metadata degrades the model's ability to appropriately weight information based on source authority and temporal proximity. The provided draft suffers from a systemic lack of structural claim traceability across all sections.

* **Missing claim\_id:** No claims in the original draft possess a compliant claim\_id. The draft relies on rudimentary superscript numbers that map to an external ledger, rather than embedding unique programmatic tracking identifiers directly with the claim.
* **Missing explicit source attribution:** In-text claims frequently omit the exact publisher or data provider within the text structure before mapping to an endnote, requiring the model to infer institutional origin.
* **Missing observation/release dates:** A significant portion of the claims present data using relative temporal markers such as "currently," "recently," or "latest" (e.g., "currently trading at $4,551.49") without assigning an explicit, absolute temporal anchor (e.g., May 15, 2026).
* **Missing claim\_type:** Claims are not explicitly tagged with the mandated allowable variables (FACT, OBSERVED\_VALUE, LABELED\_FORECAST\_OR\_ESTIMATE, SCHEDULED\_EVENT, SOURCE\_REPORTED\_UNCERTAINTY) within the text block.
* **Missing source confidence labels:** No claims feature a high, medium, or low confidence parameter to aid the model in resolving contradictory data.
* **Prohibited labeling:** The draft explicitly uses the unauthorized prefixes "INTERPRETATION:", "UNCERTAINTY:", and "CATALYST:" to introduce qualitative opinions. This is an overt violation of the acceptable claim types framework, explicitly framing qualitative, human-generated analysis as primary benchmark data.
