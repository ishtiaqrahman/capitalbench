## U.S. Macro, Inflation, Labor, And Policy Facts

- The Federal Reserve's June 17, 2026 FOMC statement maintained the target range for the federal funds rate at 3.5% to 3.75%.
- BEA reported on June 25 that May personal income rose 0.7%, disposable personal income rose 0.7%, current-dollar PCE rose 0.7%, and real PCE rose 0.3%.
- BEA reported May PCE prices rose 0.4% month over month and 4.1% year over year. Core PCE rose 0.3% month over month and 3.4% year over year.
- BEA's Q1 2026 third estimate showed real GDP grew at a 2.1% annual rate, up from 0.5% in Q4 2025. BEA also reported real final sales to private domestic purchasers at 1.7% and corporate profits from current production up $74.4 billion in the quarter.
- BLS reported May JOLTS job openings unchanged at 7.6 million and the job-openings rate unchanged at 4.6%. Hires were unchanged at 5.2 million, total separations changed little at 5.1 million, and quits were little changed at 3.1 million.
- The Conference Board reported the Consumer Confidence Index increased 0.6 points to 91.2 in June from a downwardly revised 90.6 in May. The Present Situation Index fell 3.0 points to 116.4, and the Expectations Index rose 3.0 points to 74.4. Its release said the preliminary survey period was June 1-23 and covered an extension of the U.S.-Iran ceasefire agreement.
- The Conference Board chief economist statement said consumer confidence inched up as falling oil prices provided some relief to consumer inflation fears, while perceptions of the current labor market softened; the share of consumers saying jobs were hard to get rose to 22.5%.
